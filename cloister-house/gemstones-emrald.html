Gemstones Emerald

Most highly sought after gemstones

Emerald is one of the most highly sought after gemstones on the planet. Emerald is of the beryl family, colored green by trace amounts of chromium and/or vanadium. All green Beryl is not Emerald, the natural coloring agent is key to identification. Often Emerald will have blue as a secondary color rather than yellow. All Beryl has a hardness of 7.5–8 on the 10 point Mohs scale of mineral hardness but most emeralds are highly included, so their toughness is only fair.

Emeralds, like all colored gemstones, are graded using the four C's, Color, Cut, Clarity and Carat. Color is by far the most important criterion in Emeralds. A fine emerald must have a pure green and also a high degree of transparency to be considered a top gem.

Emeralds tend to have numerous inclusions and even surface breaking fissures, thus they are traditionally oiled in order to improve their clarity. Unlike diamond, where the loupe is used to grade clarity, emerald is graded by the naked eye, a more forgiving manner due to the nature of the gem. Cedar oil is often used in this accepted practice, however, other liquids, including synthetic oils and polymers are also used. Disclosure of all treatment when an emerald is sold is normal business practice.

Colombia is by far the world’s largest producer of emeralds but many countries also have good quality emerald mines. Zambia is known to have finer emeralds than Columbia, however, all locations that produce high quality also produce a range all the way down to simple industrial quality. Thus location is important but not a guarantee.

A consumer considering a purchase of an emerald should have the help of a gemologist and even consider getting a treatment report from a reputable gemological laboratory in the case of an expensive purchase.
