Investment Current Economic Climate

Arguably the most important contributor to the risk-on and risk-off dynamic these days is the euro zone, which hosts a slow-burning crisis that provides the market with a constant stream of news flow and occasional flare-ups, as policymakers with different (and sometimes competing) interests, struggle to come up with a solution. While most investors are focused on the risks the crisis presents, one also sees opportunities. An optimist sees the opportunity in every risk, a pessimist the risk in every opportunity.

As it stands, I don't think the European policy makers are willing to acknowledge the huge dichotomy between reality and their world view. The local politics in Europe is universally much more nationalistic and locally oriented than the bureaucrats, in many cases, ‘the unelected leaders of the EU’. So these unelected leaders of the EU and the senior executives of the banks are convinced that their vision of a unified Europe is what all of the local people want. And they're plowing ahead with that vision regardless of the reality of the situation.

In the event of a breakup, investors in financial assets such as stocks and bonds need to be prepared for a period of extreme volatility, characterized by chaotic trading conditions in all markets. With debt burdens increasing in the euro zone and in the US, there is little evidence of a plan to effectively address them.

A look around the investment world one can safely say that only a few safe havens for investors remain!
